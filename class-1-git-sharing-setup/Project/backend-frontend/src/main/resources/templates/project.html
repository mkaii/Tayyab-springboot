Let's Save People

Problem

People overboard on ships not having a big enough chance of survival.

Details
Effects

  • People overboard
  • Ship Companies
  • Ship Crew
  • Passengers

Solution

MOB Seeker would detect if someone fell overboard and would deploy a boat to locate the individual, use GPS to help rescue the individual with minimal effort required.

Problem

The current technique of rescuing someone overboard is not automated and requires a big manual effort if at all the individual overboard is sighted and the survival chances of the victims are low

Currently, the process for MOB

  • The ship goes into emergency mode. The bridge will contact the Coast Guard and a search will begin. The ship will stop and the crew will try to pinpoint where and when it happened so they can start a thorough search in that area.
  • The captain also will notify other ships in the area who are then obligated to help search
  • The ship will keep searching until the Coast Guard clears it to continue its voyage to the next port.
  • The Coast Guard will continue searching, widening the radius of the search based on probable locations, conditions and ocean currents, until they feel they're unlikely to find the person

Solution

The Autonomous MOB (Man Overboard) Seeker would detect if someone fell overboard and would deploy a boat to locate the individual, provide an inflatable Life Buoy and use GPS to help rescue the individual with minimal effort required. It could potentially be scaled up to automatically retrieve the MOB individual.

  • Testing on land
  • Predicting the fastest route between two points where the destination shifts its location
  • Mapping and simulating the results in real time

Project Parts
  • Arduino - gathering GPS data
  • Java - calculating the data to find best route
  • Website - visualising the data
